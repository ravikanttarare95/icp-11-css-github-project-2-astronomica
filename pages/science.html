Space-Science:Exploring the Unknown, understanding the Known.

What is Space Science?

Space science is a broad field that explores celestial bodies, the universe, and the physical laws governing them. It includes disciplines such as:

  • Astrophysics: Studies the properties and behavior of celestial objects.
  • Astrobiology: Searches for life beyond Earth and explores the conditions necessary for life.
  • Planetary Sciences: Examines planets, moons, and other solar system bodies.
  • Cosmology:Investigates the origin and evolution of the universe.
  • Astrogeology: Studies the geology of celestial bodies.

Space science helps us understand the universe, from the tiniest particles to the largest galaxies, and paves the way for space exploration and technological advancements.

Branch of space-science are:

Astrophysics

Astrophysics, the study of the physics of the universe, emerged from astronomy in the 17th century with scientists like Newton arguing for common physical laws for celestial and terrestrial realms, and gained momentum with the development of spectroscopy in the 19th century, allowing for the study of stellar composition. Astrophysics is a branch of space science that uses the principles of physics and chemistry to study the universe, exploring topics like the formation, evolution, and properties of celestial objects, from stars and planets to galaxies and the cosmos itself.

Astrometry

Astrometry is the science of measuring the position and movement of celestial objects, such as stars and planets.

  • How does astrometry work?
  • Astrometry measures the position of a star in the sky compared to other stars.
  • It looks for a regular wobble in the star's position.
  • If a periodic shift is detected, it's likely that the star is being orbited by a planet.
