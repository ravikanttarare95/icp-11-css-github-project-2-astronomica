Astrophysics

Astrophysics, the study of the physics of the universe, emerged from astronomy in the 17th century with scientists like Newton arguing for common physical laws for celestial and terrestrial realms, and gained momentum with the development of spectroscopy in the 19th century, allowing for the study of stellar composition. Astrophysics is a branch of space science that uses the principles of physics and chemistry to study the universe, exploring topics like the formation, evolution, and properties of celestial objects, from stars and planets to galaxies and the cosmos itself.
